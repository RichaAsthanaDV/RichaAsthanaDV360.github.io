Brief: Apple is launching the AirPods Max in India — a highly premium product with a price point that demands precise audience targeting. The objective was brand awareness through programmatic video across 5 metros, with a hard brief requirement: only reach users who can afford the product. Budget was floating, meaning impression allocation needed to stay flexible based on performance. Primary KPI: match or exceed existing CTR benchmarks. Advertiser blacklist exclusions mandatory.

My approach: I structured 4 line items in DV360, each targeting a distinct audience segment — all running programmatic video on CPM at ₹80, all devices, capped at 3 exposures per week. Rather than one broad audience, separating by segment let me identify which group drove the strongest VTR and engagement, giving Apple actionable data to optimise future campaigns.

The premium income challenge: To address the "only those who can afford it" requirement, I layered high household income segments from DV360 on top of each interest audience, and applied advertiser blacklist exclusions to remove low-quality inventory. Music fans received the largest allocation (₹20,000 / 36% of budget) — for a premium audio product, music interest is the strongest proxy for both relevance and purchase intent. The competitor line (Bose/Sony fans) was kept smallest: these users have demonstrated spend capacity but existing brand loyalty, making them harder to convert at awareness stage. With a floating budget, week-one VTR data would determine whether to scale any line or reallocate.

CLIENT CAMPAIGN · DV360 · DISPLAY + VIDEO
Brita Jug Filter — Brand awareness launch, Bengaluru

Brief: Brita is a water filter brand — not a purifier. Its core USP is better-tasting water, not health or safety claims. The product hero for this campaign was the Brita Marella Jug, launching in Bengaluru targeting upper-middle-class households. Available exclusively on Amazon India. Objective: build brand awareness and drive Amazon product page visits among households likely to buy a premium kitchen lifestyle product.

Why the USP matters for targeting: Because Brita is a filter, not a purifier, the audience is not worried about water safety — they already have clean water and want it to taste better. This shifted my targeting away from health anxiety audiences (common mistake) and toward lifestyle, cooking, and premium home product audiences — people who care about the quality of their everyday experience at home.

// Optimisation strategy · weeks 1–8

Week 1–2
Baseline and brand safety check. Reviewed placement reports, excluded irrelevant or low-quality domains. Confirmed floodlight firing correctly on Amazon PDP. No budget moves until data stabilised.
Week 3–4
Audience performance review. Custom intent (L3) showed 2× higher CTR than in-market (L1) at similar CPM — shifted 10% of L1 budget to L3. Cooking contextual (L2) had strongest viewability; maintained allocation.
Week 5–6
Creative rotation and frequency review. Identified frequency saturation on L1 (avg. 4.2 exposures vs 3/wk cap — pacing issue). Reduced L1 bid floor and introduced creative rotation to reduce fatigue on the cooking audience.
Week 7–8
Remarketing scale-up. Amazon PDP visits had accumulated enough pool size to scale L4. Increased remarketing budget by 15%, reduced lookback to 7 days to focus on highest-intent recent visitors. Final CPM on remarketing: ₹65 vs ₹92 prospecting average.

Key insight delivered to client: The custom intent audience (L3) consistently outperformed the in-market segment (L1) — CTR of 0.13% vs the 0.08% India display benchmark — because Brita is a new brand in India — users searching "water filter jug" are discovering the category, not comparing known brands. For a market entry campaign, keyword-based custom intent captures active discovery intent better than broad in-market labels. Recommended making L3 the primary prospecting line for any future Brita India activity.
